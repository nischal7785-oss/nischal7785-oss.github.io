MECHANICS
1. Laws of Motion (Newton's Laws)
Newton's three laws are the foundation of classical mechanics and appear in every NDA 1 paper in some form.
First Law (Inertia)
A body remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force. Inertia is the resistance to change in motion. Inertia ∝ Mass.
Second Law
F = ma    (Net Force = mass × acceleration)
Also written as F = dp/dt (rate of change of momentum). This is the most important equation in mechanics.
Third Law
Every action has an equal and opposite reaction. Action-reaction pairs act on different bodies — they never cancel each other.
⭐ BEST SOURCE
H.C. Verma — Concepts of Physics Vol.1, Chapter 5. Read all 3 laws, do ALL worked examples. Then solve NCERT Exemplar problems.
2. Work, Energy & Power
Work = F · d · cos θ  |  KE = ½mv²  |  PE = mgh  |  Power = Work/time = Fv
Work-Energy Theorem: Net work done on a body = Change in KE. Conservation of Energy: Total mechanical energy (KE + PE) is conserved when only conservative forces act.
  • Work is a scalar quantity (dot product of force and displacement)
  • 1 horsepower = 746 watts (NDA loves this conversion)
  • Elastic collision: both KE and momentum conserved
  • Inelastic collision: only momentum conserved, KE lost
  • Perfectly inelastic: bodies stick together after collision
3. Circular Motion & Gravitation
Centripetal force = mv²/r = mω²r  |  g = GM/R²
Escape velocity = √(2gR) ≈ 11.2 km/s (Earth)  |  Orbital velocity = √(gR)
  • Banking angle: tan θ = v²/rg (no friction case)
  • Geostationary orbit: height ~36,000 km, period = 24 hours
  • g decreases with altitude and depth; g is maximum at poles, minimum at equator
  • Kepler's 3rd Law: T² ∝ R³
4. Rotational Motion
τ = Iα (torque)  |  L = Iω (angular momentum)  |  KE_rot = ½Iω²
Moment of inertia depends on mass AND distribution. For a uniform rod about centre: I = ML²/12. For a solid sphere: I = 2MR²/5. Conservation of angular momentum: if no external torque, L = constant (e.g., spinning skater).
5. Properties of Matter
  • Young's Modulus Y = Stress/Strain = (F/A)/(ΔL/L)
  • Bulk Modulus: resistance to volume change
  • Viscosity: internal resistance of fluid to flow (η). Blood is more viscous than water.
  • Bernoulli's Principle: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant
  • Surface Tension: force per unit length; decreases with temperature
  • Stoke's law: F = 6πηrv (terminal velocity of sphere in fluid)
⭐ QUICK REVISION
Oswaal NDA Chapter-wise PYQs for Mechanics. Solve all 5-year questions on Laws of Motion and Work-Energy before moving on.
OPTICS & WAVES
1. Ray Optics
Mirror: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u  |  Lens: 1/f = 1/v − 1/u  |  m = v/u
Snell's Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂  |  n = c/v
  • Critical angle: sin C = 1/n (denser to rarer medium)
  • Total Internal Reflection: angle > critical angle; used in optical fibres
  • Power of lens P = 1/f (in metres); unit = Dioptre
  • Lens maker's equation: 1/f = (n−1)(1/R₁ − 1/R₂)
  • Concave mirror: focal length negative (real is positive convention)
  • Myopia (short-sight): corrected by concave lens. Hypermetropia: convex lens.
2. Wave Optics
  • Young's Double Slit: fringe width β = λD/d
  • Constructive interference: path difference = nλ
  • Destructive interference: path difference = (2n-1)λ/2
  • Diffraction: bending of waves around obstacles
3. Sound Waves
v_sound = 332 m/s at 0°C; 344 m/s at 22°C  |  Doppler: f' = f(v±v_o)/(v∓v_s)
  • Beats: number of beats/sec = |f₁ - f₂| (max = 10 beats/sec audible)
  • Audible range: 20 Hz – 20,000 Hz. Ultrasound > 20 kHz.
  • Resonance in open pipe: L = λ/2, λ, 3λ/2... Closed pipe: L = λ/4, 3λ/4...
⭐ BEST SOURCE
NCERT Class XII Physics Chapter 9 (Ray Optics) + Chapter 10 (Wave Optics). These two chapters alone cover ~90% of NDA optics questions.
THERMODYNAMICS
Laws of Thermodynamics
ΔU = Q − W  (First Law)  |  Entropy of universe always increases (Second Law)
  • Zeroth Law: If A=B and B=C in thermal equilibrium, then A=C → basis of thermometer
  • Isothermal process: T = constant, PV = constant
  • Adiabatic process: Q = 0, PVᵞ = constant
  • Isochoric: V = constant, Q = ΔU
  • Isobaric: P = constant, Q = nCpΔT
  • Carnot efficiency: η = 1 − T₂/T₁ (maximum possible)
Kinetic Theory of Gases
PV = nRT  |  KE = (3/2)kT  |  v_rms = √(3RT/M)
  • At STP: 1 mole gas occupies 22.4 L
  • Avogadro's number = 6.022 × 10²³
  • Gas constant R = 8.314 J/mol·K
  • Boltzmann's constant k = 1.38 × 10⁻²³ J/K
⭐ BEST SOURCE
NCERT XI Chapter 11 (Thermal Properties) + Chapter 12 (Thermodynamics) + Chapter 13 (Kinetic Theory). All three are short and very scoring!
ELECTRICITY & MAGNETISM
Electrostatics
Coulomb's Law: F = kq₁q₂/r²  |  E = F/q  |  V = kq/r
C = Q/V  |  Energy = ½CV²  |  Series: 1/C = 1/C₁+1/C₂  |  Parallel: C = C₁+C₂
Current Electricity
Ohm's Law: V = IR  |  P = VI = I²R = V²/R  |  Kirchhoff: ΣI=0, ΣV=0
  • Series: R_total = R₁+R₂+R₃ (current same, voltage divides)
  • Parallel: 1/R = 1/R₁+1/R₂ (voltage same, current divides)
  • Wheatstone bridge: balanced when R₁/R₂ = R₃/R₄
  • EMF vs Terminal voltage: V = E − Ir (r = internal resistance)
Magnetism & Electromagnetic Induction
F = qvB sinθ  |  F = BIL  |  EMF = −dΦ/dt (Faraday's Law)
  • Lenz's Law: induced current opposes the change causing it
  • Fleming's Left-hand rule: motor (current-carrying conductor in field)
  • Fleming's Right-hand rule: generator (conductor moving in field)
  • Transformer: V₁/V₂ = N₁/N₂; Step-up: N₂ > N₁
⭐ BEST SOURCE
H.C. Verma Vol.2 (Chapters on Electrostatics, Current, Magnetism). Focus especially on circuits — they appear in every NDA paper. Arihant NDA for solved PYQs.
CHEMISTRY — INORGANIC & PHYSICAL
Periodic Table & Trends
  • Atomic radius decreases left→right, increases top→bottom
  • Ionization energy increases left→right, decreases top→bottom
  • Electronegativity: Fluorine is highest (4.0 Pauling scale)
  • Noble gases: He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn — inert, full outer shells
  • Most electronegative: F > O > N > Cl
Chemical Bonding
  • Ionic bond: metal + non-metal. High MP, conducts when molten/dissolved.
  • Covalent bond: non-metal + non-metal. Sharing electrons.
  • Hydrogen bond: N-H, O-H, F-H (explains high BP of water)
  • VSEPR Theory: shapes of molecules based on electron pair repulsion
  • sp³ → tetrahedral (109.5°), sp² → trigonal planar (120°), sp → linear (180°)
Important Reactions & Compounds (NDA Favourites)
  • Haber Process: N₂ + 3H₂ → 2NH₃ (high pressure, 400°C, Fe catalyst)
  • Contact Process: SO₂ + O₂ → SO₃ → H₂SO₄
  • Thermite Reaction: Fe₂O₃ + 2Al → 2Fe + Al₂O₃ (used in welding)
  • pH: acidic < 7, neutral = 7, basic > 7. pH = −log[H⁺]
  • Plaster of Paris: CaSO₄·½H₂O. Baking soda: NaHCO₃. Washing soda: Na₂CO₃·10H₂O
⭐ BEST SOURCE
NCERT Class XI Chemistry Chapter 3 (Classification of Elements) + Chapter 4 (Chemical Bonding). These are the highest-yield chapters. Supplement with Arihant NDA for trick questions.
BIOLOGY — COMPLETE
Cell Biology
  • Prokaryote vs Eukaryote: no nucleus vs true nucleus
  • Mitosis: growth/repair (4 phases: PMAT). Meiosis: gamete formation, reduces chromosome number.
  • Organelles: Mitochondria = powerhouse. Ribosome = protein synthesis. Chloroplast = photosynthesis.
  • Cell membrane: selectively permeable, fluid mosaic model
Human Systems (HIGHEST YIELD in NDA)
  • Digestive: Mouth→Esophagus→Stomach→Small Intestine→Large Intestine. Liver produces bile, Pancreas produces insulin + digestive enzymes.
  • Circulatory: 4-chambered heart. Systole = contraction, Diastole = relaxation. Normal BP = 120/80 mmHg.
  • Respiratory: Breathing rate = 16-20/min. Alveoli = gas exchange surface. Haemoglobin carries O₂.
  • Excretory: Kidney filters 180 L plasma/day, excretes 1.5 L urine. Nephron = functional unit.
  • Nervous: Brain (cerebrum, cerebellum, brainstem). Spinal cord. Neurons = nerve cells.
  • Endocrine: Pituitary (master gland), Thyroid (T3,T4 — metabolism), Adrenal (adrenaline — fight/flight), Pancreas (insulin/glucagon).
Genetics
  • DNA = Double helix. Base pairs: A-T, G-C. Discovered by Watson & Crick (1953).
  • Mendel's Laws: Law of Segregation, Law of Independent Assortment
  • Dominant vs Recessive alleles. Genotype vs Phenotype.
  • Blood groups: A, B, AB (universal recipient), O (universal donor)
  • Sex determination: XX = female, XY = male. Sex-linked diseases: haemophilia, colour-blindness.
Ecology
  • Food chain: Producer → Herbivore → Carnivore → Decomposer
  • 10% energy rule: only 10% energy transferred between trophic levels
  • Biomes: Tropical rainforest (max biodiversity), Tundra (min), Desert, etc.
  • Greenhouse gases: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, H₂O. Global warming = enhanced greenhouse effect.
⭐ BEST SOURCE
NCERT Biology Class X (Control & Coordination, Life Processes) + Class XII (Reproduction, Genetics, Ecology). These 8 chapters cover ~95% of all NDA biology questions.
ALGEBRA & SETS
Sets, Relations & Functions
  • Set operations: Union (A∪B), Intersection (A∩B), Complement (A'), Difference (A-B)
  • De Morgan's Laws: (A∪B)' = A'∩B' and (A∩B)' = A'∪B'
  • Number of subsets of a set with n elements = 2ⁿ
  • Types of functions: One-one (injective), Onto (surjective), Bijective
Quadratic Equations & Progressions
ax²+bx+c=0: roots = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2a  |  Sum of roots = −b/a, Product = c/a
AP: a, a+d, a+2d... Sum = n/2[2a+(n-1)d]  |  GP: Sum = a(rⁿ−1)/(r−1)
Matrices & Determinants
  • Matrix multiplication: rows × columns. Not commutative (AB ≠ BA in general)
  • Determinant of 2×2: |a b; c d| = ad − bc
  • Inverse exists only if det ≠ 0 (non-singular matrix)
  • Cramer's Rule for system of equations using determinants
⭐ BEST SOURCE
R.S. Aggarwal — Mathematics for NDA. Solve all exercises on Sets (Ch.1), Quadratics (Ch.3), AP/GP (Ch.6), Matrices (Ch.8). These four topics = ~25 questions in every paper.
TRIGONOMETRY
Core Identities (Must Memorise)
sin²θ + cos²θ = 1  |  1 + tan²θ = sec²θ  |  1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
sin(A±B) = sinA cosB ± cosA sinB  |  cos(A±B) = cosA cosB ∓ sinA sinB
sin 2A = 2 sinA cosA  |  cos 2A = cos²A − sin²A = 1 − 2sin²A = 2cos²A − 1
Inverse Trigonometry
  • Domain of sin⁻¹x: [−1,1], Range: [−π/2, π/2]
  • Domain of cos⁻¹x: [−1,1], Range: [0, π]
  • sin⁻¹x + cos⁻¹x = π/2 (for x ∈ [−1,1])
Properties of Triangles
Sine Rule: a/sinA = b/sinB = c/sinC = 2R  |  Cosine Rule: a² = b²+c² − 2bc cosA
⭐ BEST SOURCE
R.D. Sharma Class XI Trigonometry chapters. Trigonometry forms ~20% of NDA Maths. Focus on: identities, inverse trig, and properties of triangles. Practice 50+ PYQs separately.
CALCULUS
Limits & Continuity
lim(sinx/x) = 1 as x→0  |  lim((aˣ−1)/x) = ln a as x→0
Differentiation
d(xⁿ)/dx = nxⁿ⁻¹  |  d(sin x)/dx = cos x  |  d(eˣ)/dx = eˣ  |  d(ln x)/dx = 1/x
Chain Rule: d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x))·g'(x)  |  Product Rule: (uv)' = u'v + uv'
Integration
∫xⁿ dx = xⁿ⁺¹/(n+1) + C  |  ∫sin x dx = −cos x  |  ∫eˣ dx = eˣ + C
∫₀^π sin x dx = 2  |  ∫₋π^π sin x dx = 0
Applications
  • Maxima/Minima: f'(x)=0 and check f''(x). f''<0 = max, f''>0 = min
  • Area under curve = ∫ y dx between limits
  • Rate of change problems: distance-time, compound interest
⭐ BEST SOURCE
NCERT Class XII Maths Chapters 5-8 (Continuity, Differentiation, Application of Derivatives, Integrals). Then R.S. Aggarwal NDA for 200+ practice questions.
STATISTICS & PROBABILITY
Statistics
Mean = Σx/n  |  Variance σ² = Σ(x−x̄)²/n  |  SD = √Variance
  • Median: middle value when sorted. Mode: most frequent value.
  • For symmetrical distribution: Mean = Median = Mode
  • Coefficient of variation = (SD/Mean) × 100
Probability
P(A) = Favourable outcomes / Total outcomes  |  0 ≤ P(A) ≤ 1
P(A∪B) = P(A)+P(B)−P(A∩B)  |  Bayes: P(A|B) = P(B|A)·P(A)/P(B)
  • Independent events: P(A∩B) = P(A)·P(B)
  • Binomial distribution: P(X=r) = ⁿCᵣ·pʳ·qⁿ⁻ʳ
  • Permutations: ⁿPᵣ = n!/(n−r)!   Combinations: ⁿCᵣ = n!/r!(n−r)!
⭐ BEST SOURCE
NCERT Class XI Ch.15 (Statistics) + Class XII Ch.13 (Probability). Statistics is very scoring — easy questions but needs formula accuracy. Always attempt these first in the exam!
